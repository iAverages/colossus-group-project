Frequently asked questions

Is the colossus the first computer?

Yes, the colossus is defined as the world's first electronic computer, developed for the sole purpose of deciphering the Lorenz-encrypted messages between Hitler and his generals during the WWII.

Who invented the Colossus?

Tommy Flowers

When was it invented?

Tommy Flowers spent eleven months designing and building Colossus at the Post Office Research Station, Dollis Hill, in North West London. After a functional test, Colossus Mk 1 was delivered to Bletchley Park in late December 1943 / January 1944, was assembled there by Harry Fensom and Don Horwood, and was working in early February 1944.

Can I see the colossus?

Yes, the colossus is on display at the National Museum of Computing on Bletchley Park.
